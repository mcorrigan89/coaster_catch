THE GOAL

The goal of Coaster Catch is to be the last player to successfully flip and catch their ever-growing stack of coasters.

HERE'S THE CATCH...

To perform the flip, begin with your stack of coasters hanging off the edge of the table. With your palm facing down, tap the stack upwards with the back of your extended fingers. Allow the stack to flip 180 degrees and catch it between your thumb and fingers as shown.

THE SETUP

1. Find a surface with a hard edge to play on. Surfaces with rounded edges are not recommended.

2. It's helpful to spend at least a few minutes practicing flipping and catching a coaster, especially for new players. Once everyone is comfortable with the maneuver, continue setup.

3. Each player starts with three (3) Lifelines.
Note: To shorten play time or increase difficulty, you can start with fewer Lifelines.

4. Shuffle 100 coasters and place them facedown in a stack at the center of the play area. This will be the Community Stack.

5. Pick a player to go first using any method you like. The direction of play begins clockwise.

TURNS

Start your turn by taking a coaster from the top of the Community Stack and adding it to your Personal Stack.

There are two kinds of coasters, which will determine how to proceed:

Blank Coasters

The player simply adds the coaster to their stack and flips to end their turn.

Action Coasters

The player must perform the action displayed on the coaster before flipping their stack to end their turn. If you do not have enough coasters required to perform the action, the coaster is treated as blank. Any coasters picked up as the direct result of an action are treated as blanks.

The turn ends with the player flipping and catching their Personal Stack. If the player is successful, it is the next player's turn. If a catch is unsuccessful, that player must remove one Lifeline and end their turn. When a player fails to catch their stack and they have no more Lifelines in their possession, they are out of the game.

Note: For questionable catch attempts, group consensus determines if it was legal or not. The game continues until one person remains in the game.

ACTIONS

SKIP YOUR FLIP Add a coaster to your stack, but do not flip it. This ends your turn and reverses the direction of play.

REVERSE Add a coaster to your stack, but do not flip it. This ends your turn and reverses the direction of play.

SWAP STACKS You must trade stacks with another player.

TAKE In addition to this coaster, add the umber of coasters shown to your stack.

GIVE Including this coaster, give the number of your coasters shown to another player.

FLIP 2 Simultaneously flip one coaster in each hand. If this is not possible, perform a 360 Flip.

360 FLIP Flip a single coaster at least one full rotation.

DOGPILE Every player gives you a coaster.

HALF STACK Divide your stack in half and place one half at the bottom of the Community Stack. For odd numbers of coasters, the remainder is placed in the Community Stack.

LIFELINE Add one Lifeline to your collection.

NON-DOM Flip your stack with your non-dominant hand. This flip will end your turn.

PICK 'N' STICK Select a coaster from your Personal Stack and place it in the Community Stack anywhere you like.

THIEF Steal a Lifeline from another player.
